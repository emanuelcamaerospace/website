About

Aviation has been a lifelong passion of mine. What began as a childhood dream of becoming an airline pilot gradually evolved into a deeper curiosity about the engineering principles that make flight possible. Today, I continue to nurture that passion through flight simulation and active engagement with the broader aviation industry.

This curiosity led me to pursue admission into Portugal’s most competitive engineering program: Aerospace Engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico. After graduating high school with an average of 19.13/20, I moved from a rural village to Lisbon to begin my academic journey — a significant personal and academic transition that strengthened my adaptability and resilience.

In the summer of 2024, I completed an internship at ANAC (Autoridade Nacional da Aviação Civil), Portugal’s civil aviation authority. During this period, I developed a software tool capable of predicting UAS flight trajectories following critical failures, supporting early-stage certification processes. According to my project coordinator, the developed model has practical applicability within regulatory frameworks, subject to refinement. This experience marked my first meaningful technical contribution to the aerospace field.

During the first year of my Master’s degree, I pursued an Erasmus semester at the Technical University of Munich, where I focused on propulsion and aircraft systems engineering. Motivated to complement academic study with hands-on engineering, I joined BLUE, a student-led initiative dedicated to the design and development of micro turbojet engines for small-scale UAV applications.
